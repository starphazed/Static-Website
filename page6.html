Six

Realms of Existence

The number 6 holds significant meaning in Buddhism, symbolizing the Six Realms of Existence. These realms, also known as the Six Paths, represent the different states of existence that sentient beings are reborn into, based on their karma. The realms include Heavenly Beings (Devas), Humans, Asuras (demigods), Animals, Hungry Ghosts (Preta), and Hell Beings. Each realm reflects a particular type of suffering or pleasure, with beings moving through these realms depending on their actions and intentions in previous lives.

While the Heavenly Realm is a place of great pleasure and long life, even it is impermanent, as beings there eventually fall back into the cycle of rebirth. The Human Realm is considered the most favorable because it offers a balance of pleasure and suffering, making it ideal for spiritual growth and the pursuit of enlightenment. The lower realms, such as the Animal, Hungry Ghost, and Hell Realms, represent states of ignorance, insatiable desire, and intense suffering. In Buddhist teachings, the goal is to transcend these realms by breaking free from samsara, the cycle of rebirth, through the attainment of Nirvana.
